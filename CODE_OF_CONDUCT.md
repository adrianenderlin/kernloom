# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Kernloom aims to be a focused, respectful, engineering-driven project.

Participants are expected to:

- be respectful and direct
- discuss technical ideas in good faith
- explain disagreement with substance
- keep criticism constructive and specific
- avoid personal attacks, ridicule, or hostility
- respect different experience levels and backgrounds

---

## Unacceptable behavior

This includes, for example:

- harassment or intimidation
- insulting, demeaning, or inflammatory language
- personal attacks instead of technical discussion
- deliberate bad-faith disruption
- doxxing or sharing private information without consent
- repeated off-topic escalation after a maintainer decision

---

## Project culture

Kernloom values:

- technical clarity
- operational realism
- thoughtful disagreement
- concise, actionable review feedback
- security-aware engineering

Strong technical disagreement is acceptable.
Personal disrespect is not.

---

## Maintainer responsibility

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or other interactions that undermine a productive and respectful project environment.

They may also decline further engagement where discussion is no longer constructive.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the maintainer.

Please include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if available
- any relevant context

---

## Enforcement

Depending on severity and pattern, responses may include:

- a warning
- moderation of comments or threads
- closing or locking a discussion
- temporary or permanent exclusion from the project spaces

---

## In short

Be respectful, be precise, and argue the technical point instead of the person.
